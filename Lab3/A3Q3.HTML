Happiness

Happiness is a state of well-being marked by contentment, joy, and a sense of purpose. It goes beyond fleeting pleasure, encompassing life satisfaction and positive emotions. Research shows strong relationships, meaningful work, and gratitude are key contributors. While genetics and circumstances play a role, personal choices like kindness and mindfulness significantly influence happiness. It's not found in constant highs but in appreciating simple moments and living with intention. Ultimately, happiness is a personal journey, shaped by how we perceive and engage with life.

According to Aristotle:
"It is not a destination but a direction."
According to Dalai Lama XIV:
"Happiness is not something ready-made but comes from one's own actions."
According to Mahatma Gandhi:
"Happiness is achieved when one's thoughts, words, and actions are in harmony."

Childhood

Childhood is a precious, carefree time filled with joy and innocence. It is a period of wonder, learning, and simple pleasures, free from the worries of adult life. This stage shapes a person's character, values, and worldview, laying the foundation for their future. The memories formed during childhood often bring comfort and nostalgia later in life. It is a time of growth, discovery, and the development of essential life skills, making it a crucial and cherished phase of human development.

According to Leo Tolstoy:
"Childhood is the most important period of a person’s life, not because it determines the future but because it is the purest time of being.”
According to Ralph Waldo Emerson:
“The child amidst his baubles is learning the action of light, motion, gravity, muscular force…”
According to Graham Greene:
“There is always one moment in childhood when the door opens and lets the future in.”
